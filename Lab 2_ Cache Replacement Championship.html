In this assignment, you will implement and evaluate cache replacement policies for the Last-Level Cache (LLC) using the ChampSim simulator.

  - Part A: Implement SHIP++ as described in the paper
  - Part B: Design your own policy that outperforms SHIP++

  ---
  Part A: SHIP++ Implementation

  Implement the SHIP++ cache replacement policy as described in the following paper:

  Paper: https://www.dropbox.com/s/algx6mwxa591uoy/Ship%2B%2B.pdf?dl=1 Links to an external site. 

  Your implementation should faithfully follow the design and mechanisms presented in the paper.

  ---
  Part B: Designing a Better Replacement Policy

  Design and implement a cache replacement policy that outperforms SHIP++.

  Your policy does not need to be based on SHIP++. You are free to design any replacement strategy, as long as it is compatible with the provided simulation framework.

  This part is competitive - your custom policy will be ranked against other submissions on a leaderboard based on geometric mean speedup over LRU.
   
  Storage Budget: 2 bytes/line

  ---
  Getting Started

  Code Repository: https://github.gatech.edu/htaneja3/champsim_crc2 Links to an external site. 

  Important: Use this repository, not the latest ChampSim release. The CRC2 version has a different API.

  For detailed instructions on building, running, and implementing your policies, see:

  https://github.gatech.edu/htaneja3/champsim_crc2/blob/main/README_STUDENTS.md Links to an external site. 

  ---
  Gradescope Submission

  Submit the following files on Gradescope:

  - ship_pp.llc_repl (Your SHIP++ implementation)
  - custom.llc_repl (Your custom replacement policy)
  - report.pdf (Report for Part B)

  Report Requirements (Part B)

  Your report should include (1 Page):

  - Design: Describe your replacement policy. What is the key idea? What techniques does it use?
  - Performance: Report the IPC and speedup over LRU for each trace. How does it compare to SHIP++?
  - Storage Overhead: Calculate the total storage required by your policy (in bytes or KB). Break down the storage by data structure (e.g., RRPV bits per line, signature tables, counters, etc.)

  ---
  Resources

  - SHIP++ Paper: https://www.dropbox.com/s/algx6mwxa591uoy/Ship%2B%2B.pdf?dl=1 Links to an external site. 
  - CRC2 Website: https://crc2.ece.tamu.edu/ Links to an external site.
